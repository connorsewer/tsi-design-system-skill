# Visual Modes

Choose one primary mode per artifact. Mixing modes is allowed only when hierarchy is clear.

## Light Premium System

Default mode for 2.0.

Use:

- White and cool blue-white surfaces.
- TSI-blue CTAs, links, rails, and proof highlights.
- Hairline blue-gray borders.
- Soft brand-derived gradients.
- Refined 10-16px radii.
- Sparse, precise copy.
- Product or image artifact anchored beside the message.

Best for landing pages, product pages, decks, executive summaries, visual explainers, and most web examples.

## Warm Editorial System

Use for thought leadership, reports, high-trust campaign pages, and premium narrative sections.

Use:

- Warm paper and stone foundations.
- TSI blue for the brand/interaction layer.
- Poppins plus a restrained editorial serif lane.
- Generous margins, strong captions, and calm proof blocks.

Do not let warm neutrals replace the TSI identity. Logo, CTA, chart, and active-state color should still feel TSI-led.

## Product System Artifact

Use for mechanism visuals, data products, workflow previews, and interactive components.

Use:

- Clean control surfaces.
- UI/data mono details.
- Rails, tokens, gates, tabs, status rows, and charts.
- Motion that reveals state or flow.
- Synthetic illustrative data only.

Best for DebtNext, UAS, CollectX, PULSE, dashboards, portals, operating layers, and page hero artifacts.

## Dark Feature Panel

Use dark mode as an accent or product stage, not as the default mood.

Use:

- TSI dark blue or near-black blue canvas.
- Bright blue edge light.
- White text with strong contrast.
- Green and orange for success/exception states.
- Official white logo variant when placing brand marks.

Best for final CTAs, data moments, interactive product previews, and high-contrast proof visuals.

## Generated Visual Families

Use new generated imagery for:

- Light hero atmospheres.
- Abstract product systems.
- Editorial data monuments.
- Workflow diagrams.
- Spot icons and small accents.
- Motion-ready layers.
- Respectful human/portrait moments.

Avoid locking the system to a single prior generation style. The official duotone sketch style is one available lane, not the only lane.
