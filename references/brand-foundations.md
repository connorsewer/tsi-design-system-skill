# Brand Foundations

Use this reference for positioning, voice, sub-brand scope, and compliance-safe judgment.

## Positioning

TSI is a customer-first receivables, loan servicing, healthcare revenue cycle, and customer experience operations company. The 2.0 system should make TSI feel like a modern operating-infrastructure partner rather than a legacy collections vendor.

Core ideas:

- Revenue recovery with customer relationships intact.
- Compliance-aware operations.
- Omnichannel communications and intelligent workflows.
- Vertical expertise across ARM, Loan Servicing, CXBPO, and Healthcare RCM.
- Productized systems, analytics, and operating control.

Current tagline: `Revenue recovery, reimagined.` Italicize `reimagined` in display contexts when supported.

## Brand Families

- Parent TSI: corporate, cross-LOB, executive, enterprise.
- TSI ARM: accounts receivable management, recovery strategy, compliance, customer relationship preservation.
- TSI Loan Servicing: borrower servicing, onboarding, borrower control, servicing rails.
- TSI CXBPO: customer experience operations, right-shore staffing, AI-assisted deflection, quality controls.
- TSI Healthcare RCM: denial recovery, patient billing, revenue cycle, PULSE workflows.
- DebtNext, UAS, CollectX, PULSE: product/customer-facing systems. Use caveats when content may require legal, product, or COI sign-off.

## Voice

Write in an executive-modern voice:

- Direct and plainspoken.
- Product-literate without jargon overload.
- Premium, calm, and precise.
- Compliance-aware without sounding fearful.
- Confident, not boastful.

Prefer:

- "TSI helps operations teams see, route, and recover revenue with more control."
- "One governed workflow can replace a patchwork of vendors, spreadsheets, and exception paths."
- "The system shows where work is moving, where it is blocked, and where expert review matters."

Avoid:

- "Best-in-class results powered by cutting-edge AI."
- "Crush delinquencies."
- "Never lose revenue again."
- Exclamation points.
- Emoji.
- Shame, panic, threat, or legal-intimidation language.

## Hard Rails

- Never invent TSI facts, client results, compliance certifications, legal claims, or product capabilities.
- Never show distressed consumers, shamed borrowers, frightened patients, court threats, or aggressive collectors.
- Never show fake account, patient, borrower, payment, or client data.
- Never generate logos or marks. Place official artwork afterward.
- Use synthetic illustrative data only and label it as such when needed.

## The Main Failure To Fight

The biggest 2.0 failure mode is generic AI SaaS: interchangeable gradients, vague copy, over-rounded feature cards, decorative dashboards, and "AI brain" metaphors. Every output should have at least one TSI-specific anchor: regulated workflow, recovery operations, customer-preserving language, official asset use, productized mechanism, or vertical context.
