# Components And Patterns

Use this reference for web, product UI, and deck-like HTML work. Component Gallery is a research source for real-world patterns, not a source to copy verbatim.

## Core Primitives

Buttons:

- Primary: TSI dark blue background, white text, 6px radius, confident hover darkening.
- Accent: bright blue background, white text, used sparingly.
- Secondary: white or transparent background, dark-blue text, hairline border.

Navigation:

- Light nav is default.
- Use official logo at correct variant and aspect ratio.
- Active indicators use thin bright-blue rules or compact tabs.

Cards and panels:

- Use 10-16px radius for 2.0 premium surfaces.
- Use hairline borders and soft layered shadows.
- Avoid identical 4-up generic cards. Vary scale, content density, or visual role.

Forms:

- Labels are clear and compact.
- Inputs use white or cool surfaces, blue-gray borders, and bright-blue focus rings.
- Do not use fake sensitive form data in examples.

Status:

- Green means resolved, verified, active, complete, or improved.
- Orange means exception, warning, blocked path, leakage, or restriction.
- Avoid red unless a regulatory or destructive state truly requires it.

## High-Value Patterns

Hero module:

- Message left, visual artifact right or behind with clear safe area.
- Light premium surfaces by default.
- Official logo in nav or top-left brand position.
- One crisp CTA and one secondary proof/action.

Proof strip:

- 3-4 synthetic metrics or capability claims.
- Large Poppins Light numerals.
- Small labels and source/caveat text.

Product artifact:

- Tabs, rails, validation gates, metric cards, or timeline.
- Data is synthetic and illustrative.
- Motion can cycle state or reveal flow.

Editorial band:

- Warm paper or cool surface foundation.
- Serif accent possible for long-form pull quote or narrative block.
- TSI blue CTA/anchor remains visible.

Final CTA:

- Dark blue or light premium, depending on page arc.
- Official logo or symbol placed correctly.
- One action, not a menu of asks.

## Anti-Generic Rules

- Do not produce vague "feature card" grids without a mechanism or proof.
- Do not use a gradient just to fill empty space.
- Do not make every component a rounded rectangle with an icon in a circle.
- Do not use fake dashboards with tiny unreadable labels.
- Do not choose components because they are trendy; choose them because they clarify an operational story.
